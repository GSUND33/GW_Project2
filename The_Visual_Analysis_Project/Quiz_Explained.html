The Why Behind This Quiz

Thank you for taking our quiz and contributing to our analysis on how people see and interpret data. Now, that was a tough quiz, and it has nothing to do with how intelligent or fast you are at reading, scrolling, and zooming. The questions themselves were inherently easy ones to answer, however the visualizations being presented were not always the best choice to convey the answers. A good visualization should allow viewers to quickly consume the information and understand the main points being presented.

See below for a breakdown of the quiz and how using the right visualization can make all the difference.


This quiz consisted of only 5 questions, the only thing changed was the order of the answers and the visualizations which accompanied them.

  • Which Industry has the third highest average employment?
  • Which State has the lowest average annual pay
  • In which year did Education and Health Services first pass 35K in Average Annual Pay?
  • Are more industries paying annual wages above or below the average?
  • In which State is the county with the most highest paying jobs?

Dimension vs Measure


In the first question you were asked to identify which industry, a dimension1 had the third highest average employment, a measure2. Looking at the below bar chart it is easy to quickly identify Education and Health Services as the correct answer.




The two below images dipicted the same information in a different way, relying on your ability to quickly judge the size of each shape to determine which value was the third largest. Judging minor differences in the size of objects is typically much harder for people to do accuratley and quickly, making these visualizations a poor choice to convey the desired message.




Many people like to use pie charts to show the type of data used in this question. Although as it is easy to see from the below image, pie charts are not the best choice to show how an even realatively low number of items stack up against each other. As in the charts above, it is difficult to judge which wedges of the pie are larger than others without further information, which in a chart showing this many variables would quickly get crowded and difficult to read.






Dimension vs Measure with Location


The next question asked which state had the lowest average annual pay. Individuals answering questions such as this often like to use maps in order to depict the data geographically. However maps are best suited for conveying information with large differences between the various data points, or to show specific locations that are far enough apart to avoid overlapping. In this case as seen in these maps from the quiz, the data doesnt have enough disparity to allow readers to easily identify specific information such as the state with the lowest pay.








In this case the below bar chart was much better suited to quickly allow Pennsylvania to be identified as the answer simply by looking for the last bar in the chart.






Dimension vs Measure with Time


Depicting information relating to time is often best viewed on a line graph. The question asking the year in which Education and Health Services first passed 35K in Average Annual Pay, definetly puts this to the test. While the below line graph allows viewers to easily identify that Education and Health Services passed the 35K mark in 2012, this fact was not immediatley obvious and required the use of a legend to decipher which line to follow.





The same information can also be easily and quickly identifed in the below chart requiring you to simply find the correct industry and travers over to the correct year.




As easy as it was to use a chart, the below visual which only swaps the industry and time values used in the line graph then plots the data as rings is almost useless to a reader attempting to see how much an industry paid each year.






Comparison


The fourth question asked you to compare information in order to determine if most industires were paying annual wages above or below the average. This question was asked three times using a bar chart, a text chart, and a pie chart. All three of these visuals adequately conveyed the information needed to answer this question. However the following two charts required you to count each industry to figure out the answer, consuming precious seconds on a timed question.



This question is a great example of where pie charts shine. Unlike the above images this pie chart takes up very little space, is easy to quickly interpret and communicate a definitive message.






Measure vs Measure


The final question asked you to identify in which state was the county with the most highest paying jobs. As with the rest of the questions in this quiz, both visuals provide enough information to come to the correct conclusion. In the below scatter plot you were required to read both axis in order to identify which direction corresponded to pay and which corresponded to the number of jobs. You then had to use the legend to identify which state the dot at the furthest right belonged to, identifying New York as the state with the most highest paying jobs.





The bubble chart on the other hand despite having a large number of points which lack a label allows for a much quicker conclusion. When using this chart the reader can use the color legend and immediatley identify New York County in the state of New York as the largest dark blue bubble and therefore the state with the most highest paying jobs.
